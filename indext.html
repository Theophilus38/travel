Olumo Rock


Olumo Rock, located in Abeokuta, Ogun State, is a historic and cultural landmark that offers visitors a glimpse into Nigeria's rich heritage. Standing at 137 meters above sea level, the rock served as a natural fortress for the Egba people during inter-tribal wars in the 19th century. Today, it's a popular tourist destination where visitors can explore its ancient caves, climb the rock via accessible stairways or natural paths, and enjoy panoramic views of Abeokuta city. The site also features shrines, sculptures, and historical artifacts that highlight its cultural significance.

Ikogosi Waterfalls


Ikogosi Warm Springs, located in Ikogosi, Ekiti State, is a unique natural wonder where warm and cold springs flow side by side, meeting at a confluence but maintaining their distinct temperatures. Surrounded by lush greenery and serene landscapes, this destination offers visitors a tranquil retreat. The site has become a popular spot for relaxation, picnics, and exploration, with well-maintained walkways leading to the springs. The resort around the area also provides accommodations and amenities for those looking to enjoy a peaceful getaway in nature

Lekki Conservation Centre


Lekki Conservation Centre, located in Lagos, is a premier nature reserve and one of the city's top eco-tourism destinations. Spanning 78 hectares, this lush urban oasis is home to a variety of wildlife, including monkeys, crocodiles, and exotic birds. The highlight of the reserve is the iconic canopy walkway, the longest in Africa, offering breathtaking views from above the treetops. Visitors can also explore nature trails, relax in picnic areas, and learn about conservation efforts at the reserve. It's a perfect escape for nature lovers looking to experience the tranquility and beauty of Nigeria's natural environment right in the heart of Lagos.

National Museum, Lagos


The National Museum Lagos, located in the heart of Lagos Island, is one of Nigeria's most important cultural institutions. Established in 1957, the museum houses an extensive collection of Nigerian art, artifacts, and historical objects that reflect the country's rich cultural heritage. Notable exhibits include traditional sculptures, masks, and the famous 9th-century bronze works from the ancient Kingdom of Benin. The museum also features galleries dedicated to Nigeria's colonial history, traditional crafts, and a collection of ethnographic materials. It's a must-visit destination for those interested in exploring Nigeria's diverse cultures and historical past.

Zuma Rock


Zuma Rock, located in Niger State near Abuja, is one of Nigeria's most iconic natural landmarks. This massive monolith, often referred to as the "Gateway to Abuja," stands at about 725 meters above sea level and is easily recognizable by the face-like features that appear on its surface. Zuma Rock holds cultural and spiritual significance for the local Gwari people, and it offers visitors a striking sight along with opportunities for hiking and photography. As a symbol of strength and resilience, Zuma Rock is a must-see destination for those exploring the central region of Nigeria.
